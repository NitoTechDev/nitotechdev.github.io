Creating an Overlay for Your Cheats with Direct3D 9

Sep 14, 2025 | Direct3D 9 Overlay Writeup

Why create an overlay for your cheat?

What's the purpose of adding an overlay to your cheat? That's the question I asked myself when first learning about adding an overlay to a cheat I was creating for Skyrim.

Overlays can help the cheat developer as well as potential users of a cheat keep up with specific values or toggled cheats. Allowing for more clarity on how your hack operates and it's capabilities.

What is required to create an overlay?

Things you need:

  • 2010 Direct3D 9 SDK
  • IDE that is setup for Direct3D includes and libraries
  • Game that uses Direct3D 9
  • A DLL that you plan to inject into the game

Downloading the SDK

You can download the SDK from here: Microsoft legacy SDK download

It has already reached it's End of Life and is no longer supported. If you run into any issues installing the SDK it's because you already have a version of it installed.

You'll need to uninstall your current version and run the installer again so you can gaurentee you have DirectX 9 installed which is included with this installer

Setting up your IDE

Within your IDE of choice (I use Visual Studio, so will give instructions based on that),
you can right click on your solution and go to properties

Navigate to VC++ Directories, then edit your Include Directories to be (ENTER THIS AT A LATER TIME)

Then within that same page, you'll edit your Library Directories to be (ENTER THIS AT A LATER TIME)

Afterwards you can press apply, then press ok, and your IDE will be setup to include the Direct3D and DirectX includes and libraries!


These instructions assume you have a solution created for your cheat already.
